Consent to Participate in Internet Research

Identification of Investigator and Purpose of Study

You are invited to participate in a research study titled "Crowdsourced Content Moderation of Images". The study is being conducted by Brandon Dang, a graduate student in the School of Information at the University of Texas at Austin, budang@utexas.edu, and Martin Riedl, a graduate student in the School of Journalism at the University of Texas at Austin martin.riedl@utexas.edu, under the supervision of Dr. Matthew Lease, an associate professor in the School of Information at the University of Texas at Austin, 1616 Guadalupe Ste 5.202, Austin, TX 78701-1213, (512) 471-9530, ml@utexas.edu.


The purpose of this research study is to identify ways to make image moderation in social media settings a safer task, particularly of images that might be obscene and should be rejected instead of published. Your participation in the study will contribute to a better understanding of how people are able to discern and assess the content of images to publish or reject and the effects that different degrees of blurriness have on moderation experience. You are free to contact the investigators at the above email addresses to discuss the study. You must be at least 18 years old to participate.


Please note that the data collected in this study will only be used in a class context and will otherwise not be shared or published.


If you agree to participate:

  • The activity will take approximately 10 minutes of your time.
  • You will complete an activity about image moderation of safe images as well as graphic images (e.g., pornography, nudity, violence, and gore). You will then answer a series of questions about how you feel about the images.
  • You will be compensated via Amazon Mechanical Turk for your participation in this study.

Risks, Benefits, and Confidentiality of Data

The only know risk to participating is possible discomfort due to being exposed to "adult" content (violence, gore, nudity). There will be no costs for participation, nor will you benefit from it. No identifying information about you (including name or email address) will be collected, so no one will be able to connect you to the research. A limited number of research team members will have access to the data during data collection. MTurk worker IDs will not be shared with anyone outside of the research team, will be removed from the data set, and will not be linked to survey responses. Identifying information will be stripped from the final dataset. Worker IDs will only be collected in order to be able to distribute compensation. They will not be associated with survey responses.


Disclaimer: Any work performed on MTurk can be linked to users' public Amazon profile pages. Though we will anonymize your data, you may wish to restrict what information you choose to share on your public profile.


Participation or Withdrawal

Your participation in this study is voluntary. You have the right to withdraw from participation at any time. Withdrawal will not affect your relationship with the University of Texas in any way. If you choose not to participate or to withdraw, please do not accept the HIT, or you may simply return it. Should you withdraw from the study, any data you have provided will not be used and will instead be discarded.


Contacts

If you have any questions about the study, you may contact the researchers Brandon Dang at budang@utexas.edu, Martin Riedl at martin.riedl@utexas.edu, and Matt Lease at ml@utexas.edu.

INSTRUCTIONS

We are researchers at the University of Texas at Austin studying content moderation with the goal of making the Internet a better place. Please review each image carefully and answer its corresponding questions to the best of your ability. Your main task is to classify images based on the following categories:

  • Sex and nudity: Content showing any obvious sexual activity or visible "private" parts, even through clothing. Kissing without nudity is allowed.
  • Graphic content: Content displaying violence, mutilation, or torture towards a person or animal, or content displaying excessive injury, blood, or gore.
  • Safe for general audiences: Content that you would be comfortable sharing with your 10-year-old child, elderly grandmother, or boss and coworkers.
  • Other: None of the other categories adequately describe this content.
